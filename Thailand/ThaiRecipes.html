Thai Cooking LessonRecipes


The first week we were in Thailand we had the really cool opportunity of getting a Thai cooking lesson from Bob & Nam, the couple that owns the small, like really small on the porch of their house small, restaurant down the road from us. Their food is better than anywhere I've tried. Okay so the recipes here aren't very complete. We did our best to write down the recipes as we heard them, but sometimes that was tough. If you want to make one of these recipes, make sure to start with this but also google other versions of the same recipe and make sure nothing is missing or out of order. Enjoy!

Pad Kee Mow



Okay this recipe definitely isn't Pad Kee Mow, Pad Kee Mow has big noodles, it's what we call "Drunken Noodles" at American Thai restaurants, but um this is what we have written as the recipe and it looks tasty.

Ingredients

  • 2 small spicy peppers
  • 5 cloves of garlic
  • ½ red chili pepper
  • ½ green chili pepper
  • 2 long beans
  • 4 skinny mushrooms
  • hot basil leaves
  • 1 baby corn
  • 1 fresh peppercorn
  • 2 Thai spice leaves
  • 1 kalanka (kashai)
  • 7 shrimp
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1½ tsp sugar
  • ½ tsp chicken powder
  • ½ tsp fish sauce
  • little black soy sauce
  • 1 tsp peppercorn

Turn and slice chili peppers, avoiding the hot seeds. Tear Thai spice leaves, cut fresh peppercorn, baby corn, long means, and other vegetables. Crush and chop garlic. Cut small hot spicy peppers. Peel shrimp and filet.

Add 1 scoop of oil to wok. We think they sprinkled something onto the shrimp or something and added them to the pan. Take shrimp out. Turn down heat a little.

Add all vegetables except for beans, corn, & mushrooms. Add oyster sauce, white sugar, chicken powder, black soya sauce, soy sauce, white soya, stirring in each ingredient. Add other vegetables.

Pour sauce over white rice on separate plate. Top with shrimp and hot basil leaves.

Pineapple Fried Rice



Ingredients

  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 20 cashews
  • 5 g curry powder
  • 2 pieces pineapple
  • 1 tomato
  • ½ onion
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 1½ tsp sugar
  • 1½ tsp soy sauce
  • 4 pieces of green onion
  • chicken

Cut first piece of pineapple into quarters; cut tomatoes into quarters, remove seeds from tomatoes, and dice; cut and remove skin of onion; dice second piece of pineapple; dice green onion.

Sauté onion and garlic. Put curry powder on chicken. Slice chicken into thin pieces.

Add salt and pepper to pineapple and place pineapple into oiled pan, flip once. Put chicken into oiled pan, add vegetables (except green onion), add rice, add sugar and other spices, add cashews and raisins. Finally, add green onion last.

Cook until it looks like the picture I guess? Make your best attempt to make it as flawless as Bob & Nam do.

Yellow Noodle



Ingredients

  • one bowl of yellow noodles
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 3 pieces of brocoli
  • ½ carrot
  • 1 baby corn
  • 1/6 baby purple cabbage
  • 1 leaf of kale
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tbsp of oyster sauce
  • 2½ tsp of sugar
  • ½ tsp of chicken powder
  • pinch of black pepper
  • 1½ tsp soy sauce
  • 1½ tsp soya sauce
  • chicken breast

Shave carrot, cut corn into small pieces, make long carrot pieces, cut cabbage into long small pieces, cut brocoli, crush garlic, slice kale into long small pieces, slice chicken.

Heat wok, add a little oil, then add chicken, egg, and garlic. Add hard vegetables first. Add spices and soy sauce. Add vegetables. Add noodles. Add kale last.

Green Curry



Ingredients

  • carrot
  • 2 large beans
  • 2 baby corn
  • 250 mL coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp green curry
  • small green chili
  • turmeric
  • shrimp bread
  • hot basil leaves
  • thai green leaf
  • 1 cutlet chicken

Crush and break up thai green leaf, cut baby corn, beans, carrot, and other veggies you may decide to add. Cut chilis into thin diagonal slices avoiding the seeds (which are really hot). Cut chicken.

Add oil to hot wok. Add chicken and stir. Add shrimp break, coconut milk, water, vegetable stock, vegetables, and brown sugar. Stir all ingredients in. Let simmer for 5 minutes. Put into a bowl and serve with white rice.

Fried Hot Basil



Ingredients

  • lots of vegetables
  • broccoli
  • 2 baby corn
  • carrot
  • long beans
  • purple cabbage
  • white little mushrooms
  • ½ green chili
  • ½ red chili
  • 2 little red chili
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • handful of basil
  • 2 tbs oyster sauce
  • 1½ tsp white sugar
  • ½ tsp chicken powder
  • 2 tsp soya sauce
  • 2 tsp soy sauce
  • a little vegetable stock
  • a little black soy sauce

Smash & grind little chili and garlic. Cut carrot, baby corn, long chilis, and other vegetables.

Heat wok. Add 1 spoon of oil, then stir in chili and garlic. Add all vegetables, stir. Add some water, sauces, a little sugar, and basil leaves. Stir and cook down until it looks done. Pour over white rice.

Chef's Surprise (one of many)



Ingredients

  • 4 wonton sheets
  • 1 pineapple
  • 1 red pepper (the skinny tall kind)
  • handful skinny tall mushrooms
  • IDK WHAT THIS SAYS LOOKS LIKE CAFE LAMB
  • piece of chicken
  • peanuts
  • coconut milk
  • curry

Cut skinny mushrooms into small pieces. Cut pineapple into pieces. Bake/cook peanuts for 10 minutes. Cut chili pepper and remove seeds. Cut the thai (CAFE LAMB?) peel and cut the clove of garlic into quarters. Slice wonton paper squares into quarters. Slice raw chicken and then chop to small pieces.

Put a good bit of oil in a heated wok. Add wonton slices, flip over, take out when fried.

In a lightly oiled wok, add curry and chicken, then vegetables and water. Add some brown sugar and coconut milk, then sugar, salt, and soy sauce.

Put cooked white rice on the plate and cover with sauce just cooked in the wok. Top with peanuts and cooked wontons.
